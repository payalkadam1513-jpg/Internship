Attractions of pune city

  • Shaniwar Wada

  1. A historic Fortification and a symbol of the Maratha Empire.
  2. Shaniwar Wada a 13 storey palace of Peshwas was built by Bajirao-I, in the year 1736.
  3. It was head quarter of the Peshwas and it symbolizes Pune's culture.
  4. The structure was built giving security the highest priority.

  • Aga Khan Palace

  1. The Aga Khan Palace in Pune is renowned for its significant role in India's freedom struggle.
  2. It served as a prison for Mahatma Gandhi, his wife Kasturba Gandhi, and other prominent leaders during the Quit India Movement.
  3. Covering a sprawling expanse of 19 acres on the Pune-Ahmednagar Road is the palatial Aga Khan Palace.
  4. The Aga Khan is known as a spiritual leader, a philanthropist, and a businessman.

  • Pataleshwar Cave Temple

  1. This temple was built during the 8th century by the Rashtrakuta dynasty.
  2. Who ruled many parts of Maharashtra and Karnataka.
  3. It was carved out of a single massive rock, which makes it very special.
  4. Pallava King Narasimhavarman I, popularly known as Mamalla, built the Varaha Cave Temple as a tribute to Lord Vishnu.
